## The Adventures of Tallon the Rogue

You don't need me to tell you how good Baldur's Gate 3 is. There's not much point in me teling you how much I enjoyed it. So, instead, (because it's all I've been playing for three months and I've got nothing else to write about!) I'm going to tell you HOW I tackled my first playthrough of Larian's epic RPG. I tried something a little different, which you might want to try for yourself!

Setup and Character Creation

I basically played the game for the first time on Normal difficulty, but set myself a few extra challenges.

First, I went into the menu and disabled Kharmic Dice. This is a feature designed to "avoid streaks of success or failure". Basically, it's a way of tweaking the random number generation (RNG) to make the game seem fairer. But Dungeons and Dragons is not supposed to be fair, so I turned it off!

Next, I turned down the number of autosaves and quicksaves that the game stored, so the game only kept the 5 most recent saves, to discourage save scumming, but give me a get out clause if I had a full party kill.

After that, the idea was simple: embrace the dice rolls! If I failed any roll in the game, I just had to go with it. Even if that meant failing a strength check to open a sarcophagus and never finding out what was inside it. Even if that means that you can't persuade a companion from going down a dark path. And I was only allowed to reload saves if the whole party died in combat.

So, it's slightly more forgiving than Honour Mode, but recreates the DnD experience of some things being out of your control. I thought it would be fun. Was I right?

Tallon's Character

I was playing Tallon as a luckless adventurer. A rogue. He had no interest in magic or in saving the world, but he wasn't exactly selfish or evil either. He just wanted to get the tadpole out of his head!

Highlights from Tallon's adventure

I don't want to spoil too much of the game, but here are some of the highlights from my playthrough.

I lost a companion character early on and was unable to bring them back, so I basically missed out on their entire story (and an entire zone that I didn't want to enter without them.
I killed another of my camp followers (no, not Astarion!) in an incident with a powerful book.
My party got off their faces before a great battle.

I also spectacularly failed to romance anyone. I had my eyes on Mama K, naturally, but I didn't find her until (I think) it was too late. The only other one to show any interest had tentacles, and I'm just not that kinda guy!

Death of Lae'zel

Early on in the game, while I was exploring the ancient ruins (and before I had recruited Withers), I took a long rest. During the night, Lae'zel snuck up on me and tried to kill me. I then tried to read her mind, looking for a way to wriggle out of the situation... and rolled a natural 1! Then I tried to persuade her not to kill me, and failed the persuasion roll (bearing in mind charisma was one of my highest stats!).

We ended up in combat. I hadn't worked out that you could toggle non-lethal attacks at that point. So, I killed her and never brought her back, because when I did finally recruit Withers, he did not give me the option. I also hadn't realised that Scroll of Revivify could resurrect people after they died, I thought it was just a way to help someone who was downed recover from a distance. (Googling it, it looks like it is not possible to resurrect her if she dies at that point anyway!)

As a result, when we got to the Githyanki creche in the mountain pass, I didn't enter - as I felt the Gith would just attack me without Lae'zel in my party.

So, when I do play through the game a second time, there will be a new storyline and area to explore!

Necromancy of Thay

As I said, I had no interest in magic or in using the mindlayer tadpoles to grow more powerful. So, when I found the Necromancy of Thay book, I inspected it and noticed that it was vulnerable to radiant damage. I took it back to my camp, put it on the floor next to the campfire and used Shadowheart's Guiding Bolt on it. The book turned to ash amidst holy flames, and shadowy creatures emerged.

Fortunately, as it was in the middle of camp, ALL of my allies came rushing into combat, and we made short work of the creatures. But not before they killed Volo. And so, another companion was lost.

Volo would later leave a note in the camp - BESIDE HIS CORPSE! - to say that he had left the party and would meet up again with us in Baldur's Gate!

Drunken Assault

My companions went charging off to assault Moonrise Towers, but my crew was injured and had used up all their spells. But I didn't have enough supplies for a long rest. So, I went back to the Last Light and raided the wine cellar. I got the achievement for taking a long rest using only alcohol, and we went striding confidently - if a little unsteadily - into the end of Act II.

Into Darkness

I found a chest, deep underground, near the edge of an abyss. Shadowheart's perception check revealed that it was a trap. I tried to disarm the trap and rolled a natural 1. The chest exploded. I resisted the damage and knockback from the explosion. Shadowheart was knocked backwards and fell to her death.

That wasn't a major story beat, it was just hilarious!
